VIRAT KOHLI

Virat Kohli is a former Indian cricket captain and one of the greatest batsmen of all time, known for his aggressive style and disciplined approach. He has played for the Indian national team and the Royal Challengers Bengaluru in the IPL, and is a former captain in all three formats of the game (Test, ODI, and T20I). His career is marked by numerous records, including being the first batter to score 50 ODI centuries, and he has won several awards, such as the Arjuna Award, Padma Shri, and Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award.

ROHIT SHARMA

Rohit Sharma is an Indian international cricketer and former captain known for his right-handed batting and nicknames like "Hitman". He has captained India in all formats and led the team to the 2024 T20 World Cup victory. Sharma is celebrated for his exceptional record in limited-overs cricket, including the highest individual score of 264 in ODIs, and has also captained the Mumbai Indians to five IPL titles. He retired from T20Is in 2024 and is scheduled to retire from Test cricket in 2025.

HARDIK PANDYA

Hardik Pandya is an Indian international cricketer who plays as a right-handed middle-order batsman and right-arm fast-medium bowler. Born on October 11, 1993, he is widely regarded as one of the world's best all-rounders in white-ball cricket, and plays for the Indian national team and captains the Mumbai Indians in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Pandya is known for his explosive hitting and has achieved several records, including being the first Indian to score a Test century just before lunch.

JASPRIT BUMRAH

Jasprit Bumrah is an Indian international cricketer known for his right-arm fast bowling. He has been a key player for the Indian national team and the Mumbai Indians in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Bumrah is recognized for his unique bowling action, pace, and ability to deliver yorkers consistently, making him one of the best fast bowlers in the world. He has achieved numerous records and accolades throughout his career.
